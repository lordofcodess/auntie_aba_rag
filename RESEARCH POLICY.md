## 1.0 Introduction

The mission of the University of Ghana (UG or "the University"), is to develop world class human resources and capabilities to meet national development needs and global challenges through quality teaching, learning, cutting edge research and knowledge dissemination. As an institution that recognizes the pivotal role research plays in the fulfilment of its mandate and as a means for socio-economic transformation and societal advancement, UG intends to distinguish itself and enhance its visibility and impact by prioritizing research.

In recognition of the scope and diversity of academic and professional disciplines at UG, and the evolution of the research and innovation governance landscape, this Policy has been revised to consolidate existing processes, and strengthen Research oversight and coordination capability.

## 2.0 Aims and Objectives of the Policy

This Research Policy outlines the general framework for the development, promotion and conduct of research at UG. It specifies principles, goals, and objectives required to ensure that research carried out under the aegis of the University, or on its premises, is ethical and of high quality, and is consistent with UG's core values and global standards.

## 3.0 Purpose of the Policy

The purpose of the Research Policy is to:

1. Build on our core strengths as a centre of excellence to secure and sustain world-class competitive advantages. Our core strengths include the following: high quality teaching and research, relevant institutions, good infrastructure, and unique competencies in the arts, humanities, sciences, and emerging academic and scholarly disciplines.

*Research Policy* **5**

2. Create more awareness of the needs of our stakeholders (students, private and public sectors, government and the global community), re-orient our teaching, research, and extension activities, and harmonise synergies between disciplines to achieve operational excellence.

3. Promote innovation, relevant and cutting-edge technology, leadership development and an enterprise culture, in order to enhance the delivery of value to our stakeholders.

4. Govern its operations by the highest level of integrity, ethical standards, openness, and fairness underpinned by a reward and recognition system that is performance driven.

## 4.0 Application and Scope

This Research Policy applies to all stakeholders (employees and students of the University, including visiting researchers, adjunct faculty, post-doctoral fellows and honorary appointees).

## 5.0 Policy Principles

The Research Policy is based on the following principles:

1. Assurance of academic freedom, creativity, and innovation.
2. Promotion of quality research that meets the highest standards.
3. Ensuring that research meets global and national development goals and priorities.
4. Ensuring that research meets health, safety, and well-being standards.
5. Upholding highest standard of ethical conduct in research.
6. Ensuring Intellectual Property emanating from research is beneficial to society.
7. Promoting good practices in data acquisition, storage, and management.
8. Effective dissemination of research output.
9. Effective support in resource mobilization for research.

## 5.1 Assurance of Academic Freedom, Creativity, and Innovation

1. University of Ghana’s core mandate of teaching, research, and extension service depends on the existence of an atmosphere in which freedom of inquiry - thought, creativity, innovation, expression, publication, and peaceable assembly - is given the fullest protection. Such protection include freedom from institutional orthodoxy, and from internal and external coercion. The diversity of scholarly endeavour that University members are supposed to engage in can only be realized if the University creates a conducive, nurturing and enabling environment.

2. Proposed mechanisms for the assurance of academic freedom, creativity and innovation include:

i) Providing a conducive intellectual space to deliberate on research ideas;
ii) Promoting respect for all branches of knowledge;
iii) Providing suitable infrastructure for research;
iv) Protecting the rights of faculty, staff, and students to conduct research;
v) Promoting optimal faculty to student ratios and balance between teaching, research, and extension activities;
vi) Encouraging research capacity building and mentoring at all levels.

## 5.2 Promotion of High-Quality Research that Meets Global Standards

1. The University of Ghana affirms its dedication to engaging in world-class research. It considers research a top priority as reflected in its vision, mission and strategic plan. Beyond disciplinary-specific scholarly pursuits, the University shall encourage research collaboration and networks with national and international agencies and institutions as well as individuals or philanthropists.

2. The University will expand, and diversify its range of research activities through the following:

i) Promotion of scientific and academic partnerships;
ii) Recognition and reward system for research excellence;
iii) Fully integrating postgraduate and post-doctoral research with faculty research activities;
iv) Promotion of collaborative research training of postgraduate students;
v) Independent monitoring and evaluation of the quality of research;
vi) Building of faculty capacity to access national and international collaboration/funding;
vii) Setting out clearly the respective responsibilities of institutions involved in collaborative research.

## 5.3 Ensuring that Research meets Global and National Development Goals and Priorities

1. The University will proactively develop and pursue where possible, a research agenda targeted at addressing national and global development needs and priorities including the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Besides prioritisation and alignment of research with national and global priorities, the University acknowledges the right of its members to engage in other forms of research for knowledge generation, provided such pursuits are consistent with the core values and mission of the University.

2. The University will achieve this through the following:

* i) Promotion of academic, private and public partnerships to identify areas of research priority in support of national and global development.
* ii) Influencing the development and implementation of national policies and programmes in all areas of knowledge generation.
* iii) Proactively engaging government to ensure adequate and sustainable funding for the University's research agenda.

## 5.4 Upholding Highest Standards of Ethical Conduct of Research

1. All research carried out shall comply with institutional, national, and international guidelines and principles. UG recognises the importance of research, so it will ensure the protection of the dignity, rights, safety and well-being of human participants, prevent cruelty to animals, and demand respect for the environment in all research carried out under its auspices.

2. The University also recognises that Research ethics encompasses broader issues such as, avoidance or prevention of misconduct, plagiarism, data forgery, misuse of University resources and conflict of interest, among others.

*Research Policy* **9**

3. To uphold highest standards of ethical conduct of research, the University will:

i) Maintain an institution-wide system for ethical approval;
ii) Mandate the established ethics and Research Committees / Boards, to review and oversee proposed research (involving human and non-human participants) and research on the environment;
iii) Institute mechanisms to promote continuous or regular training, guidance, and certification in research ethics and practices for all staff and students, in accordance with established standards;
iv) Maintain robust procedures and mechanisms for handling research misconduct.

## 5.5 Ensuring that Research Meets Health, Safety and Well-being Standards

1. The University will put into place structures and mechanisms to ensure that research is conducted safely in line with applicable institutional and national regulations and international safety standards.

2. Towards this end, the University will:

   i) Provide regular safety training and supervision, and safeguard the physical and mental wellbeing of all researchers.

   ii) Establish mechanisms to ensure that requisite approvals (e.g., regulatory) are obtained where such are required.

## 5.6 Ensuring Intellectual Property Emanating from Research is Beneficial to Society

1. UG will hold all rights to all Intellectual Property conceived, created, discovered, or reduced to practice, by any employee or student of the University, during their association with the University, unless otherwise stated.

2. Employees and students are required to disclose to the University, any Intellectual Property owned prior to appointment or enrolment at the University.

3. Employees and students are required to disclose any Intellectual Property created during the period of employment or enrolment to the University prior to public disclosure.

4. To achieve this, the University will:

* i) Maintain an institution-wide system for Intellectual Property.
* ii) Mandate the established Intellectual Property Committee to provide guidance on Intellectual Property emanating from research, innovative and creative activities undertaken by employees and students of the University.
* iii) Make rules and produce guidelines as appropriate for the disclosure, protection, development, commercialisation, and use of (including the distribution of royalties arising from) any Intellectual Property.

## 5.7 Good Practices in Data Acquisition, Storage, and Management

1. The credibility of research findings depends on appropriate data acquisition, good record keeping and data management.

2. Thus, the University will put in place a system for:

* i) Acquiring, storing and retrieving research materials/data in a robust and confidential manner in line with national and international guidelines and standards.
* ii) Managing research data/ material in line with institutional and national policies, and with requirements of applicable contractual agreements.
* iii) Ensuring timely submission of research progress reports to responsible institutional, national and international funding agencies.

## 5.8 Effective Dissemination of Research Output

1. The results of scholarly research undertaken at UG will be disseminated in an open and timely manner to the broader scholarly community, the public and other stakeholders in keeping with UG’s mission. In addition to dissemination in academic publications, critical target audiences for research findings include policy and decision makers, the private sector as well as civil society organizations. To reach all its audiences, it may be necessary to present research findings in formats which are understandable and accessible, such as non-traditional forms of presentation.

2. **In line with this, the University will:**

i) Create an enabling environment for dissemination of research output in credible and reputable academic databases and recognised outlets.

ii) Protect the right to publish, disclose and disseminate research results and methods within the limits of applicable ethical, privacy and confidentiality norms and regulations.

iii) Support the development and sustainability of institutionally affiliated journals.

iv) Outline a standard approach for acknowledgement of institutional affiliation on all research outputs, in line with best practice.

v) Support training of researchers in appropriate research communication, dissemination and translation techniques and methods.

vi) Encourage active faculty and student participation in public events such as lectures, seminars, workshops, exhibitions, and fairs.

vii) Require research publications to be made available to the UG Institutional Repository.

viii) Streamline academic authorship practices based on recognised criteria and within the tenets of academic freedom.

ix) Promote the integration of research carried out at UG into its teaching processes.
