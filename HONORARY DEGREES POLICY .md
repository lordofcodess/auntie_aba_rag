### *Policy on Honorary Degrees*

## A. Scope

This policy applies to honorary degrees conferred by the University of Ghana.

## B. Nominations and awards

### B.1 Frequency of awards

Not more than two ceremonies within any five-year period can be held for the purpose of awarding Honorary Degrees. Exceptions are to be made for the award of an honorary degree to deserving Aggrey-Fraser-Guggisberg Lecturers.

### B.2 Honorary Degrees

#### B.2.1 Awarders of the Honorary Degrees

Honorary Degrees are awarded by the University Council on recommendation of the Academic Board. Honorary Degrees seek to recognise extraordinary individuals who represent the University’s vision, mission and values. Honorary Degrees are conferred *honoris causa*, “for the sake of honour” and are awarded at the University of Ghana normally as one of three types: Doctor of Science, *honoris causa* (DSc), Doctor of Letters, *honoris causa* (DLitt) and Doctor of Laws, *honoris causa* (LLD).

* Doctor of Science, *honoris causa* (DSc) is given to persons with outstanding contributions to the Sciences.

*   Doctor of Letters, honoris causa (DLitt) is given to outstanding individuals in specific disciplines in the Humanities.

*   Doctor of Laws, honoris causa (LLD) given to persons with outstanding service to academia, the University or humankind at large.

Other types of honorary degrees may be awarded on the recommendation of the Academic Board with approval from Council.

## B.2.2 Conditions for use of the honorary degree

Recipients of an honorary degree cannot use the title for the purposes of work or obtaining employment. Thus, recipients may list their honorary degree in their curricula vitarum under ‘Awards and Honours’, but not under ‘Education’ or ‘Academic Qualifications’. In addition, honorands are not to use the title ‘Dr’ in their name.

## B.2.3 Committee to Select the Honorary Degree Recipients

The Committee to recommend the honorary degree recipients is an ad-hoc committee set up by the Vice-Chancellor for the purpose of selecting the recipients. The Committee consists of seven members, comprising at least one member from each of the four (4) colleges. The seven-member Committee shall be set up ten (10) months before the date of award.

## B.2.4 Nominators of Honorary Degree Recipients

Nominations may be put forward by members of the University Council, Heads of Academic Departments, Centres and Institutes (on behalf of their Department, Centre or Institute), Deans (on behalf of their School),

## B.2.5 Categories of Awards

### a. Distinguished Scholarship

This category is limited to academics in Ghana or elsewhere who have made substantial contributions to a specific academic discipline. The body of work of such individuals should be exceptional and recognised beyond the confines of the institutions to which they belong.

### b. Contribution to Education

This category applies to individuals whose ideas and practices substantially improve the field of primary, secondary or tertiary education in tangible ways.

### c. Alumni of whom we are Proud

This category is limited to alumni of the University of Ghana who excel in their chosen profession or career and as a result, contribute to advancing their profession for the greater good of humankind.

### d. Contribution to Public Service

This category applies to individuals who make substantial contributions to public service locally

**e. Contribution to Industry**

This category applies to individuals who make substantial contributions to their chosen profession or career in tangible ways.

**f. Institutional Builder (limited to employees of the University)**

This category is limited to current or former employees of the University who develop an initiative or programme that outlives them and that is of immense benefit to the University. Such personal initiatives may include winning and managing large research grants, soliciting funds that provide postgraduate training support, soliciting funds to establish a new unit of the University or the development of new flagship training programmes.

**g. Outstanding Innovator**

This category is limited to scientists or creative artists whose inventions or ideas improve and enrich human lives.

### B.2.6 Criteria for Selection of Awardees

Nominees shall fulfil one or more of the following criteria. They should have:

* made outstanding personal contributions to the work and development of the University.

* made outstanding contributions to scholarship,

* made outstanding contribution to scientific and technological innovation and discovery.

## B.2.7 Honorary Degrees for Aggrey-Fraser-Guggisberg Lecturers

The speaker for the Aggrey-Fraser-Guggisberg (AFG) Memorial lectures may be considered for the award of an honorary degree in one of the seven categories listed in B.2.5. That individual’s dossier shall be assessed by the Honorary Degree Committee. For this award, the Vice-Chancellor shall set up a three-person committee to deliberate on the award of an honorary degree to the selected lecturer. The committee shall make a recommendation to the Business and Executive Committee, which shall also make a recommendation to the University Council, for a final decision to be taken.

# B.2.8 Exemptions for the award of Honorary Degrees

Persons who fall in the categories below are not eligible to be nominated for the award of an honorary degree:

a. Current political office holders

b. Employees of the University (on regular or post-retirement contract)

c. Current Members of Council

d. Former Principal Officers (Chancellor, Chair of Council, Vice-Chancellor and other Senior Officers such as Pro Vice-Chancellors, Registrars and Provosts) are not automatically eligible to receive the honorary degrees but may be considered for an award in any of the categories).

e. Honorary Degrees Committee members (while they serve on the committee)

f. An honorary degree shall not be awarded in exchange for a financial contribution or a gift to the University.

g. Individuals who nominate themselves for an award

h. Individuals who have declined a previous award

*Additionally:*

a. Honorary degrees shall not be awarded posthumously.

b. Being a founding Dean or Director of an academic unit shall not be enough grounds to be awarded an honorary degree. However, a founding Dean or Director may be nominated for the award of an honorary degree based on an outstanding initiative that outlives him/her and that is of immense benefit to the University.

## B.2.9 Nominations Process*

The Registrar shall issue a notice to members of the University community in late May of the year preceding the awards for submission of nominations by the end of August.

The first notice shall be followed by two reminders, one at the end of June and another at the end of July. There shall be a dedicated electronic process for the receipt of nominations.

***The nomination and selection process shall be varied for the Aggrey-Fraser-Guggisberg Memorial lecturer as stated in B.2.7**

## B.2.10 Selection Process

The selection process will be managed by the seven-member Honorary Degrees Committee. The Committee shall first meet to screen nominations and ensure that each one of them does not fail the exemptions test listed in B.2.8. Then the Committee

shall vote independently based on the documents submitted.

Nominees who score five (5) out of seven (7) shall be shortlisted. After the screening process, shortlisted nominations shall not exceed fifteen (15). In instances where the first screening shortlist is more than 15, the committee would have to undertake a second round of voting to reduce the number to 15 or less.

Each applicant must receive a minimum of 5 votes to be considered. Ideally, for each of the 7 categories, there shall be a maximum of two people.

* The Selection Committee shall determine which category of the three honorary degrees (Doctor of Science, honoris causa (DSc), Doctor of Letters, honoris causa (DLitt.) and Doctor of Laws, honoris causa (LLD) to be awarded to each recipient.

* There shall be no obligation to fill every category if an ideal candidate is not found.

* The review process shall be completed by 31st December of the year preceding the awards and the information passed on to the Vice-Chancellor for submission to Academic Board by that deadline.

* Nominations shall be forwarded to the Academic Board two (2) weeks before its meeting.

* Academic Board using the approved processes for voting shall vote on each nominee. Nominees who obtain more than fifty (50) percent of votes cast shall be recommended to Council.

* The Registrar shall communicate the Council's final decision to successful nominees at least one month before the ceremony.

### B.2.11 Confidentiality in the nomination and selection process

Confidentiality must be maintained as far as possible by all parties regarding all stages of the process, with the exception of any public statements issued by the Registrar and/or the Public Affairs Directorate in consultation and agreement with the Vice-Chancellor. Throughout the nomination and selection process, members of the Committee shall be prohibited from contacting nominees for any information. This prohibition is to avoid possible embarrassment that may ensue if the nominee’s proposal does not receive adequate support.

## C. Revocation of Honorary Degrees

### C.1 Purpose

Honorary degree holders embody excellence as well as the values of the University. They personify and reflect the mission of the University. Thus, the University of Ghana expects that the actions and behaviours of

12 Honorary Degrees Policy

recipients of honorary degrees will be consistent with its vision and values and will not bring the name of the University into disrepute. Though diligent efforts will be made to select recipients, it is recognised that information may subsequently come to light or events may occur after conferment of the honorary degree which may tarnish the reputation of the University. This section outlines the process of revoking such an honorary degree when it becomes necessary.

## C.2 Conditions for revocation

The University may revoke an honorary degree if the recipient has engaged in conduct that:

a. is inconsistent with the values and mission of the University of Ghana.

b. undermines the accomplishments that were cited as the basis of the award.

c. harms the reputation of the University of Ghana.

d. is criminal or unethical.

## C.3 Procedure for Revocation

Honorary Degrees may be revoked by Council on recommendation of a five-member Investigations Committee set up by the Vice-Chancellor.

(a) Requests to review the award of an Honorary Degree may be submitted by faculty, students

Honorary Degrees Policy 13

or other members of the university community or organisations external to the University. Proposals must be submitted in writing to the Registrar. To deter frivolous requests, anonymous submissions will not be allowed. The identity of the individuals concerned, however, will be protected as far as possible. Requests for review must be supported with evidence.

(b) The Registrar will refer the case to the Investigations Committee set up by the Vice-Chancellor for further investigation and due diligence, including inviting the honorary degree holder to appear before the Committee. Based on its investigations and available evidence, the Investigations Committee will make recommendations to the Academic Board. The Academic Board will review the report of the Investigations Committee and make recommendations to Council on revocation or otherwise.

(c) Council will review the recommendation of the Academic Board and determine whether there are sufficient grounds for revocation of the honorary degree.

(d) The decision of Council is final and there is no right of appeal. Council through the Registrar will then inform the award holder and the individual who reported the issue about the outcome of its decision.
